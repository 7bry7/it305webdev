About PLP

PLP Mission

To provide an inclusive and transformative education that empowers students to succeed in an ever-changing world. Through community engagement, we foster a culturally diverse and globally aware learning environment equipping students with the knowledge, skills, and values necessary for total human development.

PLP Vision

To provide accessible, quality education that empowers students to become leaders and innovators in their communities and professions.

PLP Core Values

Academic Excellence, Cultural Identity, Peace Education, Sustainable Development, Social Equity and Responsibility, and Global Competitiveness.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the admission requirements?

Admission requirements include:

  • PSA Birth Certification
  • Certified True Copy of Grade 11 Report Card, or Form 138 (for SHS Graduates)
  • Certified True Copy of Form 137
  • 2 pieces passport-size picture with white background
  • Proof of residency and/or identity, such as Valid ID of student and parent/guardian
  • General Average/Grade Report​

When is the application deadline?

The application period for new applicants starts January 6, 2025, and ends March 14, 2025. During this window, documents should be submitted through the official means advised, such as the Registrar's Office or designated application portals.


Early applications are encouraged as spaces are limited.

What is the tuition fee?

No Tuition Fee!

Students do not pay tuition because PLP participates in CHED's UniFAST program and other local government subsidies, which cover both tuition and often miscellaneous fees for qualified applicants.


PLP is under the jurisdiction of the Commission on Higher Education (CHED) in the Philippines.

Do you offer scholarships?

As soon as the student is enrolled in the Pamantasan, he/she is automatically considered for the CHED Unifast scholarship.


The UniFAST scholarship, mandated by Republic Act No. 10931 (“Universal Access to Quality Tertiary Education Act”), provides free tuition and other school fees for eligible college students in public universities like PLP
